Course & Moratorium Period

What is a moratorium?
A "repayment holiday" — you don't pay EMIs while studying. Interest accrues on disbursed amounts starting from the month after each disbursement. The moratorium covers your course period plus a grace period (usually +6 or +12 months). EMI starts after the moratorium ends.

Course start and moratorium start are the same. Set the moratorium end to your course end + 6 or 12 months (varies by bank). Disbursements can happen even before the course start.

Disbursement Schedule

Banks release money in installments — usually each semester or year. Disbursements can happen even before the course starts. Interest starts accruing immediately upon each disbursement.

💡
Why disbursements matter: If ₹5L is released in Jan and ₹5L in Aug, you pay interest on only ₹5L for those 7 months — not the full ₹10L. Getting disbursements later saves interest during moratorium.

During the Moratorium

Even though you're not paying EMIs, money isn't free — interest accrues every month. Here's how to configure it.

Interest During Moratorium

Some banks ask you to pay simple interest monthly during the moratorium. This reduces the amount added to your principal at EMI start. Interest is calculated on disbursed amounts and any unpaid capitalized interest, starting IMMEDIATELY.

🧮
Formula: Monthly interest = (Outstanding principal & unpaid interest × Annual rate%) ÷ 12 (charged immediately from the month of each disbursement)
Impact: If you pay this out of pocket each month, your EMI after moratorium will be significantly lower because no interest gets added to principal.
₹

Pre-payments During Moratorium

Optional lump-sum payments you can make anytime during the moratorium. They first clear accrued interest, then reduce your outstanding principal — directly shrinking your future EMI.
